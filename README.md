## Concept

A cycle, fertility, and perimenopause tracker whose AI insight engine runs entirely on-device — no server to subpoena, breach, or sell data from, verifiable in airplane mode.

## Target User & Payer

Women 18-45 who churned from Flo/Clue after the $59.5M settlements and post-Dobbs subpoena fears; secondary wedge: perimenopausal women 35-55 ignored by fertility-focused incumbents.

## Why Edge AI Is Structural (not decoration)

iOS: AFM 3 Core (3B) with @Generable guided generation turns free-text/voice symptom logs into structured entries; Dynamic Profiles swap between logging, cycle-forecast, and fertility-Q&A modes; AFM 3 Core Advanced image input (iPhone 15 Pro+) reads ovulation/pregnancy test strips from photos. Android: Gemini Nano Prompt API on flagships, bundled Gemma 3n E2B via LiteRT-LM on mid-range. Structural, not decorative: reproductive data in any cloud is now a legal liability (Meta CIPA verdict, discovery requests), so the core promise — 'we cannot hand over what we never have' — is only true with zero data egress, proven via airplane-mode operation and published network audits.

## Why Now (2026 timing)

Trust in cloud period trackers collapsed in 2025-26 while the category remains a proven mass market; WWDC26's AFM 3 image input just made on-device test-strip reading possible; Guideline 5.1.2(i) consent friction now actively penalizes cloud-AI competitors at App Review.

## Proposed Monetization

Free manual logging; hard paywall with 7-day trial on the AI layer (pattern reports, symptom-cluster detection, forecasts, doctor-visit summaries) at $39.99/yr or $89.99 lifetime. Zero marginal inference cost makes the lifetime SKU safely profitable — a price structure cloud rivals structurally cannot match. 100K paid ≈ $4M ARR at ~100% gross margin.

## Competition & Gap

Flo and Clue are legally radioactive cloud services that cannot pivot — their analytics/ad businesses depend on the cloud pipeline. Apple Cycle Tracking is private but has no conversational insight layer, symptom NLP, or perimenopause focus. Stardust markets E2E encryption but still processes server-side. Nobody offers genuinely local AI insights.

# Evaluation (multi-agent adversarial review)

## Monetization Analysis — score 8/10

This is a rare combination: a proven mass willingness-to-pay category (Flo alone did ~$275M revenue in 2025 at a $1B+ valuation; Clue charges ~$40/yr and grosses ~$1M/mo) plus a live, verifiable trust catalyst. The $59.5M Flo/Google/Flurry settlement received preliminary approval in April 2026 with claim forms going out to the class during Selene's launch window — millions of women will literally receive legal notice that their period data was sold, which is free category-wide marketing for a 'we never have your data' alternative. The Aug 2025 Meta CIPA jury verdict makes the 'cloud reproductive data = legal liability' thesis factual, not speculative. Demonstrated switching behavior exists: Stardust gained 300K+ installs in a single weekend on privacy framing alone, then was caught with undisclosed third-party SDKs (Privacy International) — proving demand for the promise and that no incumbent credibly delivers it. The on-device AI angle is structural differentiation, not garnish: it enables the airplane-mode proof and a profitable lifetime SKU cloud rivals can't match. Why not 9-10: (1) the most privacy-motivated users historically choose free local-only apps (Euki, Drip, Periodical, Monthly are $0/nonprofit), creating price-floor pressure on exactly the wedge audience; (2) Apple Cycle Tracking is free, E2E-encrypted, and pre-installed — a good-enough default for many churners; (3) cycle-forecast accuracy without server-side population data is a cold-start risk, and any fertility/contraception claims invite FDA scrutiny (Natural Cycles needed clearance); (4) the perimenopause secondary market is real but still small (~$345-500M globally) and fragmented; (5) trust brands take time — one bad network-audit headline kills the company. Net: solid 8 — proven payers, credible wedge, perfectly timed, with execution and pricing-floor risks.

## Recommended Revenue Model

Recommended: freemium with a hard paywall on the AI insight layer, priced at $39.99/yr (anchored just below Clue's ~$40/yr and Flo Premium's ~$50/yr — undercutting incumbents while signaling 'real product, not hobby app') with a 7-day trial, plus a $79.99-89.99 lifetime SKU. The lifetime tier is the strategic weapon, not just a price point: zero marginal inference cost makes it ~100% gross margin, it converts the privacy-skeptical buyer who distrusts subscriptions for the same reason she distrusts clouds, and Flo/Clue structurally cannot match it because their COGS includes cloud inference and their business models include data monetization. Keep manual logging free forever (trust builder + App Store rating volume). Gate behind the paywall: pattern/symptom-cluster reports, cycle and perimenopause forecasts, test-strip photo reading, voice logging, and especially the doctor-visit summary PDF — the highest perceived-value artifact for the 35-55 perimenopause wedge, who have the highest disposable income in the category. Run perimenopause as a separate marketing funnel (separate App Store keywords, landing pages) into the same SKU. Realistic path: Flo converts roughly 4-6% of its 77M MAU; Selene capturing just 1-2% of the multi-million-member settlement class as paid users yields 100-200K subscribers = $4-8M ARR. Avoid monthly-only pricing (churn-heavy in this category) and avoid any ad or data-partnership revenue permanently — the entire brand is the absence of it. Time launch PR to the October 2026 final settlement hearing and claim-payout news cycle.

## Adversarial Review — strongest case AGAINST (verdict: weakened)

(1) MARKET, NOT TECH, IS THE WEAK POINT. The 'trust collapse' cohort is mostly a press narrative: the $59.5M settlement covers 2016-2019 conduct, and Flo KEPT GROWING through the 2021 FTC action, Dobbs, and the 2025 verdict (~70M+ MAU, profitable). Privacy-first local trackers already exist free (Euki, Drip, Apple Cycle Tracking with E2E-encrypted Health data) and hold negligible share — direct evidence the privacy wedge does not convert at scale. Worse, a brand-new unknown brand asking for the most sensitive data class has a trust cold-start problem: 'we're private' is literally what Flo said. (2) THE THREAT MODEL IS PARTLY THEATER. Post-Dobbs prosecutions (Burgess, Celeste Burgess cases, etc.) came from device seizures, Facebook DMs, and search history — not tracker subpoenas. The data still lives on a seizable phone and, unless the user enables Advanced Data Protection, in an iCloud backup Apple CAN decrypt for law enforcement. Airplane-mode demos don't prove future non-egress; StoreKit, crash reporting, and OS sync all create network paths. The marketing claim outruns the security reality, and the exact audience being targeted is sophisticated enough to notice. (3) MODEL QUALITY HITS THE PAYWALLED FEATURE HARDEST. The free tier (logging) is what 3B models do well; the $39.99 tier (insight, Q&A, summaries) is what they do worst. AFM 3 Core is ~3B, ~2-bit quantized, ~4K context, with guardrails that have a documented history of flagging reproductive/sexual-health content — expect refusals mid-fertility-Q&A and a fight with App Review 1.4.1 (medical apps) over hallucinated fertile-window advice, which is functionally contraception failure risk. Doctor-visit summaries over 6+ months of logs strain a 4K context. Cycle forecasting itself needs a Bayesian model, not an LLM — the LLM is decorative for the headline feature. The test-strip reader is built on a false premise: AFM 3 image input requires Core Advanced (20B sparse), which ships on current high-end devices only, NOT iPhone 15 Pro as claimed — and faint-line LH interpretation is a calibration-sensitive CV task a general VLM does unreliably; a wrong pregnancy-test read is a catastrophic failure and edges toward SaMD regulatory territory. Android is worse: Nano Prompt API covers ~140M flagships; the mid-range fallback means bundling 2-3GB of Gemma weights with thermal/RAM pain on exactly the devices the mass-market 18-45 demographic owns. The product promise fragments across device tiers. (4) SHERLOCK RISK IS REAL AND DATED: Apple is shipping Mulberry-derived AI features into the Health app during the iOS 27 cycle, already has wrist-temperature ovulation estimates, cycle-deviation alerts, and E2E Health storage. 'Private on-device cycle insights' is Apple's stated lane; Apple gets the trust halo for free that Selene must rent. The defensible residue is perimenopause depth and Android. (5) DISTRIBUTION IS THE KILLER CONSTRAINT: Meta/Google restrict reproductive-health ad targeting, removing the category's main paid channel; ASO for 'period tracker' is owned by Flo's UA budget; the privacy story earns one press cycle, not a funnel. 100K paid at $40/yr behind a hard paywall against free Flo and free Apple Health implies ~1-2M organic installs with elite conversion — heroic. WHY NOT KILLED: the structural logic survives the attack. Zero-egress AI insights are impossible via cloud API by definition; the Meta verdict's $7-8B exposure genuinely freezes incumbents' ability to ship cloud AI on this data; zero marginal inference cost makes the lifetime SKU real; perimenopause is authentically underserved; and the structured-extraction core is squarely within 3B-model competence. A disciplined team that ships iOS-first, treats the LLM as a structuring/summarization layer over deterministic Bayesian forecasting, replaces VLM strip-reading with a dedicated small CV model, and leads with the perimenopause wedge has a real-but-niche business — closer to $1M ARR lifestyle scale than the $4M ARR pitch.

## Recommended Tech Stack

iOS (iOS 26+, iPhone 15 Pro+ floor): SwiftUI; FoundationModels framework — SystemLanguageModel with @Generable guided generation for free-text/voice symptom → structured entry, Tool calling into a local stats engine; deterministic hierarchical Bayesian cycle/ovulation model in Swift (no LLM) for forecasts; SpeechAnalyzer/SpeechTranscriber (iOS 26) for on-device ASR instead of Whisper; test strips via a dedicated compact Core ML vision model (Vision framework strip detection + custom CNN classifier trained on LH/hCG strip datasets) — do NOT depend on AFM 3 Core Advanced image input given its high-end-only device floor; local RAG over a curated, citation-pinned ACOG/NICE-derived content pack using a small on-device embedding model (EmbeddingGemma-class via Core ML) to ground fertility/perimenopause Q&A and cap hallucination; GRDB/SQLite with NSFileProtectionComplete, backup exclusion by default + Advanced Data Protection guidance; StoreKit 2 only network surface; zero third-party SDKs; publish reproducible network audits (mitmproxy capture + App Privacy Report). Android: Kotlin + Jetpack Compose; ML Kit GenAI Prompt API (Gemini Nano) on supported flagships; fallback Gemma 3n E2B / Gemma 4-class via MediaPipe LLM Inference API on LiteRT-LM, weights delivered through Play Feature Delivery (on-demand, not in base APK); whisper.cpp small or offline SpeechRecognizer for ASR; same strip-reading model exported to LiteRT; Room + SQLCipher. Shared: Kotlin Multiplatform core for cycle math, Bayesian forecaster, schema, and content pack so insight logic is identical across platforms; ship Android as logging-first with AI tier gated to Nano-capable devices at launch.
