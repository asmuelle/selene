The promise

Why on-device matters

We cannot hand over what we never have.

Reproductive-health data sitting in someone else's cloud has become a real legal liability. Selene's answer is the simplest one: keep it on your phone, and keep it that way — provably.

  • Zero data egress

    The insight engine runs locally. There is no Selene server holding your logs — so there is nothing to breach, subpoena, or quietly hand over.

  • Encrypted, excluded from backups

    Your records are stored encrypted on-device and kept out of cloud backups, so they don't leak through a sync you forgot about.

  • No ads, no data partnerships — ever

    Selene is paid for by the people who use it. Your cycle is never a product, an audience segment, or a line item.

Questions

The two that matter most.

Does my data ever leave my phone?

No. Selene's insight engine runs entirely on-device, and your records are stored encrypted and excluded from cloud backups. There is no Selene account and no Selene server holding your data. You can confirm it yourself: switch on airplane mode and the app keeps forecasting and narrating as normal — because nothing was ever being sent.

Is this contraception or medical advice?

No. Selene is a tracking and reflection tool. It estimates your cycle and fertile window as predictions with ranges — it does not prevent pregnancy and should not be relied on to. It helps you notice patterns and organize your own history for a conversation with your doctor. It is not a diagnosis and not a substitute for professional medical care.

Why does on-device matter for a period app?

Your most private data shouldn't live on someone else's server. Cloud period trackers have faced privacy scrutiny and settlements over how reproductive data is stored and shared. Selene's view is simpler: the safest data is the data we never collect. We can't lose, sell, or surrender what never leaves your device.

How are forecasts calculated?

By a deterministic statistical engine — transparent math over your own logged history, not a generative chatbot guessing. That's why every estimate comes with a range and a confidence level instead of a single overconfident date.
